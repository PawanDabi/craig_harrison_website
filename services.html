How We Work

A process built on clarity and conviction.

We guide every client through a structured sequence — from the first conversation to the moment you step into a finished building. Each stage is led with transparency and precision.

01

Initial Consultation

We meet on site or in studio to understand your vision, constraints, and ambitions. No charge. No obligation. Just an honest conversation.

02

Site and Brief Analysis

A thorough appraisal of the site: topography, planning context, orientation, neighbouring buildings, and the particular character of the place.

03

Concept Design

We present a design concept — sketches, moodboards, and early spatial diagrams — to establish the architectural direction before committing to detail.

04

Planning and Approvals

We prepare and submit all planning documentation. We handle listed building consents, conservation area applications, and pre-application discussions.

05

Technical Design

Detailed construction drawings, material specifications, and contractor coordination packages — everything required to build to the highest standard.

06

Construction and Handover

We remain on site throughout construction — inspecting, resolving, and ensuring the built work matches the designed intent at every stage.

01

Planning Drawings

A complete set of drawings prepared and submitted to the local planning authority — fully compliant, clearly annotated, and ready for approval.

2D Floor Plans

Dimensioned plans showing room layouts, wall positions, openings, and spatial relationships at each level of the building.

Sections & Elevations

Vertical cuts and external faces showing heights, roof profiles, window positions, and material zones across all facades.

Site & Location Plans

Ordnance Survey based drawings showing the site boundary, orientation, neighbouring buildings, and relationship to the street.

Existing vs Proposed

Side-by-side comparison drawings showing the current state and the proposed changes — required for most planning applications.

Planning Statement

A written document explaining the design rationale, policy compliance, and contextual justification for the proposed development.

Heritage Drawings

Specialist drawings for listed building applications — recording existing fabric and clearly identifying proposed interventions.

02

3D Visualisations

Photo-realistic imagery and spatial studies that allow you to see, experience, and refine your building before a single brick is laid.

Exterior Renders

High-resolution images showing the building from key viewpoints — daylight, dusk, and seasonal conditions to understand presence and materiality.

Interior Renders

Room-level visualisations showing light, material, furniture, and spatial quality — giving a true sense of how each space will feel.

Aerial Perspectives

Bird's-eye views showing the building in context — useful for planning submissions and understanding site coverage and massing.

Animated Walkthroughs

A moving camera path through the building — ideal for client presentations, planning appeals, and communicating spatial sequence.

Material & Lighting Studies

Comparative renders testing alternative material combinations and lighting scenarios so you can make confident design decisions.

VR-Ready Models

Immersive virtual reality models allowing you to walk through the designed space at 1:1 scale before construction begins.

03

Material Specifications

A curated and detailed material schedule that removes ambiguity from the build process — every surface, finish, and product precisely defined.

Stone & Masonry

Specification of natural stone, brick, and block — including supplier, grade, coursing pattern, joint finish, and fixing method.

Timber & Cladding

Species, grade, treatment, profile, and fixing details for all structural and decorative timber — internal and external.

Glass & Glazing

U-values, frame types, glass specification, and hardware for all windows, doors, rooflights, and structural glazing elements.

Metal & Structural

Steelwork, aluminium cladding, copper roofing, and bespoke metalwork — with finish, gauge, and fabrication requirements.

Finishes & Surfaces

Internal wall, floor, and ceiling finishes — plaster, render, screed, tile, and joinery — with product references and colour schedules.

Sample Boards

Physical material sample boards presented at key design reviews so you can see, touch, and approve every material before ordering.

04

Contractor Coordination

We manage the relationship between design and construction — selecting the right contractors, protecting quality on site, and keeping every stage on track.

Tender Packages

A complete set of drawings, specifications, and schedules issued to contractors for competitive pricing — structured for clear, comparable returns.

Contractor Selection

Interview, assessment, and recommendation of shortlisted contractors — based on quality, programme, price, and track record.

Site Inspections

Regular visits throughout construction to review progress, inspect workmanship, and issue instructions — protecting the design intent on site.

Snagging & Quality

A detailed snagging inspection at practical completion — identifying and resolving every defect before the project is formally handed over.

Progress Reporting

Regular written reports keeping you informed on programme, costs, decisions, and any issues arising on site — no surprises.

Contract Administration

Formal administration of the building contract — certifying payments, issuing instructions, and managing variations throughout the build.

05

As-Built Documentation

A complete and accurate record of the finished building — everything you need for future maintenance, alterations, or resale.

Record Drawings

Updated floor plans, sections, and elevations reflecting the building as actually constructed — including any changes made during the build.

O&M Manuals

Operation and maintenance manuals for all building systems — heating, ventilation, electrical, and specialist installations.

Warranties & Certificates

Compiled guarantees, structural warranties, building regulation certificates, and specialist system certifications in a single organised pack.

Handover Pack

A clearly structured digital and physical handover pack — containing all drawings, specifications, product data sheets, and contact information.

Photographic Record

A professional photographic archive of the completed project — for your records, for planning purposes, and for future publications or sale.

BIM Model

A final 3D building information model of the completed structure — useful for future alterations, extensions, or facilities management.

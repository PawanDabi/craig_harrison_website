The Practice

About

A London-based studio with a commitment to architecture that is quietly resolved — buildings that listen before they speak.

Craig Harrison

"I believe the most important thing an architect can do is listen — to the site, to the client, to the light, to the silence."

Craig Harrison is a London-based architect with over fifteen years of experience in residential, commercial, and cultural architecture across the United Kingdom and Europe. Born in Somerset and educated at the Bartlett School of Architecture (BA Hons) and the Royal College of Art (MA Architecture), Craig founded his practice in 2009.

His work has been recognised internationally for its restraint and precision — a consistent commitment to material honesty, spatial clarity, and the quiet poetry of well-resolved form. He is equally at home designing a modest single-storey addition as he is a large-scale public building.

Craig was shortlisted for the RIBA Stirling Prize in 2021 for The Atrium Gallery in Edinburgh, and his work has been featured in Wallpaper*, Dezeen, the Architectural Review, and the Financial Times Weekend Magazine.

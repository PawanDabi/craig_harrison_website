Design Philosophy

Architecture is the art of shaping space through light, material, and human experience.



We believe that every structure is a conversation between the built and the natural — guided by restraint, material honesty, and the quiet dignity of well-crafted form.

Every detail considered. Every space purposeful.



We have worked across the full spectrum of architectural scale — from a modest extension to a major public building. In each case, the approach is the same: begin with the site, understand the brief deeply, and design with absolute conviction.

Our projects are not statements. They are solutions — each shaped by its particular place, its purpose, and the people who will inhabit it for years to come.

The Architect

Craig Harrison

Craig Harrison is a London-based architect with over fifteen years of experience in residential, commercial, and cultural architecture across the United Kingdom and Europe.

Educated at the Bartlett School of Architecture and the Royal College of Art, Craig founded his studio in 2009 with a commitment to architecture that is quietly resolved — buildings that listen before they speak, that earn their place in the landscape.

His work has been recognised by the RIBA, featured in Wallpaper*, Dezeen, and the Architectural Review, and was shortlisted for the Stirling Prize in 2021.
